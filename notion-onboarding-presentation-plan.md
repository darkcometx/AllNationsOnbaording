# **Slide 3**

## **What is Notion?**

“Notion is like a digital notebook mixed with Google Docs, Trello, and a planner — all in one app.”

You can use it to:

- Write notes and documents
- Create to-do lists
- Plan projects with teams
- Store information and files
- Organise everything in one place

---

# **Slide 4**

## **Simple analogy**

“Imagine a smart digital workspace where you can build your own:”

- notebook
- calendar
- task manager
- company wiki
- project tracker

**Why people like it:**

- Everything is customisable
- Works for individuals and teams
- Keeps everything organised
- Easy to collaborate

# **Slide 5**

## **Why are we moving to Notion?**

- Less scattered communication
- Fewer forgotten tasks
- Easier collaboration between departments
- Faster onboarding for new staff & volunteers
- One place for church knowledge
- More clarity, less confusion

**Goal:** Spend less time searching for information and more time serving people.

# **Slide 6**

## **BEFORE vs WITH NOTION**

### **BEFORE NOTION**

- Docs spread everywhere
- Important messages get buried
- Duplicate files and confusion
- Hard to track who is doing what

### **WITH NOTION**

- One searchable workspace
- Shared visibility across teams
- One latest version of everything
- Clear ownership and tracking

# **Slide 7**

## **Everything is a block**

In Notion, everything is a **block**.

A block is any piece of content:

- Text
- Images
- Checklists
- Videos
- Tables

You can:

- Move blocks around
- Rearrange pages visually
- Build layouts like LEGO pieces

---

# **Slide 8**

## **Pages are your digital notebooks**

- Pages can hold anything: text, images, tasks, databases
- Pages can contain other pages (like folders within folders)
- Everything is searchable instantly

**Think:** One giant connected notebook for everything in church life.

---

# **Slide 9**

## **How this works in real life**

**Sunday Service Planning**

Task list → Assigned teams → Notes & files attached → Live updates → Everyone sees the latest version

Same system, everyone working from one source of truth.

---

# **Slide 10**

## **Same info, different views**

One database can be viewed in different ways:

- Table → detailed spreadsheet
- Board → workflow stages
- Calendar → schedule view
- List → simple overview
- Gallery → visual cards

**Think of it like different camera angles of the same information.**
