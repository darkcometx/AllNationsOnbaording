Notion for Finance

How Finance uses Notion

💰

Annual Budget Tracker

Every department's allocation — budgeted, spent, and remaining
▼

A Notion database with each department as a row, tracking allocated budget, actual spend to date, and remaining balance — using formula properties to calculate automatically. Department leads can see their own budget without needing to email Finance for an update.

The Finance teamspace is private, but you can share specific filtered views with department leads so they see only their own allocation — not the whole org's finances.

What this looks like in Notion
Source: notion.com/help
💰 Budget 2026 — Department View
Production — £12,000 / £8,240 spent On track
Events — £18,000 / £16,100 spent Watch
Facilities — £9,500 / £4,300 spent On track
Generations — £6,000 / £2,800 spent Underspent
💡 Notion's formula properties can sum totals, calculate percentages, and flag over-budget items automatically. It's not a full accounting system, but for visibility and oversight it's excellent.
🧾

Expense Database

Every expense logged, categorised, and receipt attached — no missing receipts
▼

A database where every church expense is logged: amount, date, category, department, paid by, approved by, and receipt (uploaded directly to Notion or linked from Google Drive). Staff can submit expenses themselves with a Notion form — the request lands in your database ready for review.

Filter by department to see all events spending. Filter by month for reconciliation. Filter by "Approved = No" for your action list. Everything you need, instantly filterable.

What this looks like in Notion
Source: notion.com/help
🧾 Expense Log — May 2026
Catering — BBQ event £340 — Ify E. Approved
AV hire — Youth retreat £820 — Josh Pending
Printing — Sunday bulletins £45 — Naomi Pending
Cleaning supplies — £78 — Sarah Approved
💡 Create a staff-facing expense form that feeds into this database. Staff fill in the form on their phone straight after a purchase — receipts attached, category selected, submitted. No paper forms.
📄

Invoice & Payment Log

Every invoice received — due date, amount, status, and supplier
▼

A log of all invoices received: supplier, invoice number, date received, amount, due date, payment date, and status (Received / Approved / Paid). A formula property automatically flags invoices that are overdue.

No more invoices missed because they arrived in an email that got buried. Everything that comes in gets logged — and you can see at a glance what's outstanding this week.

📄 Invoice Log
Sparkles Cleaning Co. — £480 — Due 1 Jun Paid
AV Solutions Ltd — £1,200 — Due 28 May Awaiting approval
Print Express — £95 — Due 30 May Unpaid
💡 Set a date reminder on each invoice for 5 days before the due date. Notion notifies you in time to process payment — no more late payment situations.
🏦

Fund & Grant Tracker

Every grant, fund, and restricted income — managed and reported
▼

A database of all funds, grants, and restricted income All Nations receives: funder name, amount, purpose, start and end date, reporting requirements, and reporting deadlines. Ensures grant conditions are met and reports are submitted on time.

Link each fund to the expenses drawn from it — so you can demonstrate spend against fund purpose in seconds when a funder asks for accountability.

💡 Grant reporting deadlines set as date properties with reminders mean you're never caught off guard by a report due next week that you haven't started.
📊

Monthly Financial Report Template

A standardised summary that takes minutes to produce — not hours
▼

A Notion template for your monthly financial report — income summary, expenditure by department, budget vs. actual, notable variances, and forward look. Fill in the data (which is already in your other databases), add commentary, and you have a clean report to share with Andrew and the trustees.

Storing all past monthly reports in one database gives you a searchable archive and makes year-on-year comparison straightforward.

💡 Share monthly report pages directly with Andrew in Notion — no emailing PDFs, no wondering if they received it, no version control confusion. One live page, always current.
📞

Supplier & Payment Contact Register

Every supplier's bank details, contact, and payment terms — secure and accessible
▼

A private, Finance-only database of all supplier contacts: company name, contact person, email, phone, account number, sort code, payment terms, and any notes. The kind of sensitive data that should never be shared via WhatsApp — but needs to be findable when you need it.

Notion's private teamspace settings mean this stays exactly where it should: accessible to you and key signatories, invisible to everyone else.

💡 Keep this database strictly private. In Notion, private teamspaces are invisible to anyone not explicitly invited — this is the right home for sensitive financial contact data.
🚀
